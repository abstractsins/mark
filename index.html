I'm an assistant professor in the Department of Political Science at Marquette University. I study politics at the intersections of international law, domestic law, and human rights, with a particular focus on accountability for violations of human rights and the laws of war.

My book, Criminalizing Atrocity: The Global Spread of Criminal Laws against International Crimes, was recently published by Oxford University Press. My work has also been published in Law Society Review, Human Rights Quarterly, and the European Journal of International Law. My research has been supported by the National Science Foundation, and I was previously guest researcher at the Peace Research Institute Frankfurt in Germany.

I'm currently working on a large research project that examines the decades-long persistence of torture in the Chicago Police Department and efforts to achieve justice for the survivors.
